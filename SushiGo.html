Sushi Go Go Go

Cooking Simulation Prototype
Unity Programmer & Gameplay Designer Team Size: 3 ~2 Weeks Unity Engine

Mission Brief (Overview)

Sushi Go Go Go is a fast-paced cooking simulation prototype where players prepare sushi under time pressure. You take the role of a new sushi apprentice on their first day of work.

Gameplay Demo: Lunch Rush!
Task: Cut fish, assemble sushi, and plate dishes as quickly and accurately as possible.
Focus: Precision-based cooking interactions, rewarding accurate cuts and efficient workflow.
Inspiration: Cooking Mama (exploring physical interaction systems).

Manual (Controls)

Primary Playable Version (Mouse-based):

LMB Slice Fish
RMB Grab/Place

Interactable Items: Knife, Fish, Rice balls, Plates.

Experimental Feature: Leap Motion gesture support was prototyped (Finger slice, Pinch grab) but mouse remains the main stable input.

Workflow (Gameplay)

  • 1. Cut the fish
  • 2. Combine fish with rice
  • 3. Plate the sushi

Repeat until shift ends

Balance Speed vs Precision

Wage Calculation (Scoring)

Points represent wages earned during the shift.

Perfect Cut+15
Good Cut+10
Acceptable Cut+5
Incorrect Cut0
Assembly/Plating+5 ea
Example Perfect Sushi: 25 Points

Chef's Notes (Design & Feedback)

Design Philosophy

  • Precision-Based: No auto-cooking. Rewards control.
  • Physical Interaction: Manual actions simulate the real station feel.

Playtest Feedback

Easy to understand, good visuals.

Knife placement initially confusing (Improved in iterations).

Iterations: Added tutorials, sound feedback for cutting, and UI for money/time to improve readability.

Future Menu (Expansion)

  • More Fish: Salmon, Tuna, Eel, Tamago.
  • Orders: Specific requests (e.g., "2 Salmon Nigiri").
  • Torch Cooking: Aburi style (searing).
  • Co-op: Multiplayer kitchen roles (Cutter, Rice Maker, Plater).

End of Shift Report (Reflection)

Tutorials are key: Players must understand the task immediately.

Scope Management: Starting simple allowed us to polish the core loop within 2 weeks.

Tech Balance: Innovative inputs (Leap Motion) are exciting, but stable fallbacks (Mouse) are essential for reliability.
