Core Experience

A single-player third-person action-stealth demo. The core loop is dual survival logic from size-shifting: the teapot form handles scouting and stealth, while the human form handles high-risk breakthroughs.

Built in 12 days as a prototype to validate the core mechanic and level experience: two rule sets inside the same space, timing windows for progression, and stealth routes shaped by light and shadow.

Design Logic

Level Type

A linear progression level. Players start in the storeroom, collect key items in sequence, and reach the exhibit platform.

Progression Conditions

  • Storeroom key
  • Exhibit corridor key
  • Main hall ID card

Form Switching

Key: Tab. Switch between teapot and human form. Teapot is safer for stealth; human form enables mental attack but increases exposure risk.

Mental Attack Window

In human form, hold Left Mouse to perform a mental attack. Enemies enter a 30-second stagger when mental value reaches zero - both a reward and strict time pressure.

HP Rules

  • Teapot and human HP pools are separate.
  • Human HP zero: forced back to teapot, no future human switch.
  • Teapot HP zero: fail state ("Shattered Porcelain").

Enemy Behavior

Guard #2 joins patrol only after Guard #1 is attacked for the first time, making first aggression a key difficulty spike.

Story and Walkthrough

Story Setup

The protagonist is a once-beloved teapot spirit, now abandoned in a storeroom and fading from memory. One night, another teapot spirit proposes an identity swap at the exhibit platform.

Play Flow

  • Exit storeroom and obtain the first key.
  • Observe patrol routes using teapot form.
  • Use human form to trigger stagger and loot key items.
  • Cross laser corridor and reach final platform.
  • Success ending: "To the Light". Failure ending: "Shattered Porcelain".

Designed but Not Fully Implemented

A right-click teleport prototype and a multi-path environmental puzzle system were validated but cut from the core demo due to production scope and collision stability risks in tight indoor spaces.

Future Direction

Cultural Design Approach

Cultural elements should not only be "seen" as decoration, but also be perceived and used as functional parts of level design.

In daytime, the museum is an orderly curated space; at night, it becomes a shared field where spirit-objects coexist and the boundaries of time and space begin to loosen.

A practical next step is the "traditional costumes and jewelry corridor": patrol routes circle around key display cabinets, lights focus on exhibits, and shadow zones naturally support stealth traversal.

Next Level Concept

In Level 1 ("To the Light"), the protagonist chases the spotlight on the display platform. In Level 2, that goal shifts toward real natural light.

Structurally, the game moves from closed corridor progression to vertical spaces and route choices.

Lightweight AI spirit NPCs can be introduced as one-time helpers (briefly distracting patrols or opening temporary passages) without adding heavy system burden.

Final objective: under pressure from layered mechanisms and multiple choices, the player truly escapes the museum and sees the first beam of natural light.
